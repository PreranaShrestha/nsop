Our Story

Who We Are

The Nepali Community Society of BC (NCSBC) is a non-profit organization dedicated to serving the Nepali diaspora in British Columbia. Founded in 2008, we have grown from a small group of passionate individuals to a thriving community of over 2,500 members.

Our mission is to preserve Nepali culture, support newcomers, and foster connections among Nepalis across BC. We organize cultural events, educational programs, and community support initiatives throughout the year.

🎯 Our Mission

To empower Nepali community members in BC through cultural preservation, mutual support, and civic engagement.

👁️ Our Vision

A thriving, inclusive Nepali community in BC that celebrates its heritage while contributing to Canadian society.

Our Journey

History of NCSBC

From humble beginnings to a thriving community organization.

2008

NCSBC Founded

A group of 15 Nepali families in Vancouver came together to form the Nepali Community Society of BC, with a vision to support Nepali newcomers and preserve cultural heritage.

2010

First Major Cultural Event

NCSBC organized its first large-scale Dashain celebration, bringing together over 200 community members. This marked the beginning of our annual cultural festival tradition.

2013

Settlement Support Program Launched

Recognizing the growing number of Nepali newcomers, NCSBC launched a comprehensive settlement support program with volunteer mentors and resource guides.

2016

Nepali Language Classes Begin

To help second-generation Nepali Canadians connect with their roots, NCSBC started free Nepali language classes for children and adults.

2019

1000+ Members Milestone

NCSBC reached the milestone of 1,000 registered members, reflecting the rapid growth of the Nepali community in BC.

2022

Digital Transformation

NCSBC launched online platforms including the NCSBC Slack workspace and email list to better connect community members across BC.

2025

2500+ Members & Growing

Today, NCSBC serves over 2,500 members across British Columbia, with chapters in Vancouver, Surrey, Burnaby, Richmond, and beyond.

Governance

Bylaws & Election Matters

Our organization is governed by transparent bylaws and democratic processes.

📋 Bylaws

Article 1: Name & Purpose +
The organization shall be known as the Nepali Community Society of BC (NCSBC). Its purpose is to serve the Nepali community in British Columbia through cultural, educational, and social programs.
Article 2: Membership +
Membership is open to all individuals of Nepali origin or those with a genuine interest in Nepali culture and community. Annual membership fees apply as determined by the Board.
Article 3: Board of Directors +
The Board shall consist of a minimum of 7 and maximum of 15 directors elected by the membership. Directors serve two-year terms and may be re-elected for one additional term.
Article 4: Meetings +
The Annual General Meeting shall be held within 6 months of the fiscal year end. Special meetings may be called by the President or by petition of 20% of members.
Article 5: Finances +
The fiscal year runs from January 1 to December 31. Financial statements shall be audited annually and presented to members at the AGM.
